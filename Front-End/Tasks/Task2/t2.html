What is a website?

A website is a collection of interconnected web pages that are typically accessible through the Internet via a web browser. Each web page within a website can contain a variety of content, such as text, images, videos, and interactive elements, designed to provide information, services, or entertainment to users. Websites are hosted on web servers and are identified by unique domain names, which serve as their addresses on the Internet. Users can navigate a website by clicking on hyperlinks, which allow them to move between different pages and sections. Websites can serve various purposes, including personal blogs, business profiles, e-commerce platforms, educational resources, and social networking sites, making them a fundamental aspect of the modern digital landscape.
